Who we are

We are an interdisciplinary research team investigating the privacy and security aspects of online proctoring tools in higher education internationally.

Our research team combines expertise in the following fields:

  • Educational research from the Graduate School of Education and Human Development, the George Washington University
    • Dr. Ryan Watkins (PI)
    • Ani Meliksetyan (Graduate Research Assistant)
  • Usable security and privacy from the School of Engineering and Applied Science, the George Washington University
    • Dr. Adam J. Aviv (PI)
    • Dr. Lucy Simko (Postdoctoral Fellow, GWUSEC LAB)
  • Computer systems and the law from the University of Melbourne
    • Dr. Shaanan Cohney (CI)
    • Elisa Shioji (CAIDE Graduate Researcher, Melbourne Law School)

Project

This research project centers on the institutional decision-making regarding adoption of online proctoring in higher education institutions. It covers of the three thrust areas: the other two concentrating on students’ and educators' perception of the security and privacy aspects of online proctoring services. This project is supported by funding from the U.S. National Science Foundation.
